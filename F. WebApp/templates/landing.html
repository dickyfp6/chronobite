Chronic Diseases
Chronic diseases are long-term health conditions that require ongoing management and lifestyle modifications. Proper nutrition plays a crucial role in disease prevention and management.
Global Impact
According to the World Health Organization, chronic diseases account for 71% of global deaths. Proper dietary management can significantly reduce disease progression and improve quality of life.
Diabetes Type 2
A metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels. Affects 450+ million people globally. Dietary management focusing on controlled carbohydrates and stable blood glucose is essential.
450+ Million Affected Globally
Hypertension
Persistent elevation of blood pressure affecting 1.3 billion people. The "silent killer" requires strict sodium management and heart-healthy nutrition. 10.8 million annual deaths linked to hypertension.
1.3 Billion People Affected
Cardiovascular Disease
The leading cause of death globally with 17.9 million annual deaths. Requires careful management of fats, cholesterol, and sodium intake. Dietary intervention is critical for prevention and recovery.
17.9 Million Annual Deaths
Hyperlipidemia
Elevated cholesterol levels significantly increase cardiovascular risk. Affects 1 in 3 Americans. Managing LDL cholesterol and triglycerides through diet is essential for disease control.
1 in 3 Americans Affected
Chronic Kidney Disease
Progressive loss of kidney function affecting 10% of global population. Requires strict management of protein, sodium, potassium, and phosphorus. Dietary modification slows disease progression.
1.2 Million Annual Deaths
